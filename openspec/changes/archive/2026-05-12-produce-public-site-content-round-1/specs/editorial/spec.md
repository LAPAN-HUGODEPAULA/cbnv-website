# Editorial Standards

# Purpose
Establish editorial guidelines and linguistic standards for public-site copy to ensure professional, consistent, and accurate communication.

## ADDED Requirements

### Requirement: Brazilian Portuguese editorial standard

Public-site copy SHALL be written in clear Brazilian Portuguese.

#### Scenario: Public copy is inspected

Given a public copy block exists  
Then it SHALL use clear Brazilian Portuguese and avoid unnecessary jargon.

### Requirement: Terms to avoid

Editorial guidelines SHALL list terms and claims to avoid.

#### Scenario: Guidelines are inspected

Given the editorial guidelines exist  
Then they SHALL prohibit unsupported scale claims, exaggerated promotional language and workshop references unless confirmed.

### Requirement: Consistent terminology

Editorial guidelines SHALL define preferred terms for repeated concepts.

#### Scenario: Preferred terms are inspected

Given the editorial guidelines exist  
Then they SHALL define preferred names for event, theme, venue, registration, submissions, proceedings and previous editions.

### Requirement: Content handoff

The content-production change SHALL identify unresolved factual questions.

#### Scenario: Open questions exist

Given factual information remains unknown  
When content docs are inspected  
Then open questions SHALL be listed with owner/status where possible.
